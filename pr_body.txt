💡 **What**
Enforce a 64KB limit on JSON header lines and a 1MB limit on `data_length` and `payload_length` fields when parsing the Wyoming protocol in `wakeword.py` and `wakeword_bench.py`.

🎯 **Why**
To prevent Denial of Service (DoS) attacks via unbounded memory allocation. A malicious or malfunctioning server could previously withhold a newline character or send massive length fields, causing the application to consume memory until it crashed.

⚠️ **Risk**
Low. The limits chosen are exceptionally generous for the Wyoming protocol, which typically sends very small JSON blobs and short audio chunks.

🔬 **Verified**
Ran local tests (`pytest`) and linters (`ruff check`) to confirm no regressions.

📎 **Scope**
`linux/blitztext/wakeword.py`
`linux/blitztext/wakeword_bench.py`
